# Understanding Checks: Foundation Models

## Check Questions
1. Can the learner explain what a foundation model is and why it changed AI development?
2. Can they describe what tokens are and roughly how they map to words?
3. Do they understand the cost difference between input and output tokens?
4. Can they explain what a context window is and why it matters?
5. Can they name the major providers and at least one strength of each?
6. Can they describe the intelligence/cost/speed tradeoff in their own words?
7. Given a use case (e.g., "high-volume customer support classification"), can they reason about which model tier to start with?
8. Do they understand the difference between open and closed models?

## Completion Criteria
- The learner can explain foundation models at a conceptual level without jargon
- They understand tokens as the unit of pricing, context, and speed
- They grasp the tradeoff triangle and can apply it to model selection
- They know the major providers and their relative strengths
- They have a "start small, upgrade as needed" mental model for cost management
- No confusion about what context windows are or why they matter
